For anglers

Fish the moving water.

Slack tide is dead water, and every angler on the beach knows it. The question was never if the water will move — it's whether you'll be standing there when it does.

RipCat marks the swing on a curve you can scrub with a thumb, backs it with water temperature from the buoy you actually fish near — with a 7-day trend, so you know if the bite's been building before you burn the gas — and wraps it all in a dark UI that won't torch your night vision at 4 a.m.

  • Water temp on every buoy pin. Tap the NDBC buoy off your spot: 61°F right now, the week's trend, wind and wave history.
  • Moon, solunar, and the swing. Moonrise and moonset with bearings, phase, and the tide movement — the whole picture, one screen.
  • Every spot in your rotation. Unlimited favorites: the jetty, the flats, the pier, the secret one. Synced to your iPad at home.
  • The calendar on the tackle-shop counter? That's us. Print a month of tides with sunrise and sunset, and tape it to the garage fridge.
  • No bars, no problem. Tide and buoy data are cached for the spots where cell service goes to die.

No app catches fish. But moving water beats slack water, and you deserve to know which one you're driving to.

For boaters & sailors

Everything you'd check, before you drop the dock lines.

You know the drill: one government site for tides, another for the marine forecast, a third for the buoy — all in tables built in 1997, on a phone with one bar at the harbor mouth.

RipCat puts the same official data on one screen: NOAA tide predictions you can scrub to the minute you actually transit, NWS Small Craft Advisories painted on the map with a day-by-day timeline, and live buoy wind, waves, and pressure from outside the harbor.

  • Small Craft Advisories, on a timeline. Scrub the week and watch advisories sweep in and clear, day by day — before you promise the family a Wednesday sail.
  • The full marine forecast, one tap away. Outlook by day at a glance, then every period — winds, seas, wave detail — in the NWS's own words.
  • Your harbors, in order. Home slip, the fuel dock, the island anchorage — unlimited favorites, synced across iPhone, iPad, and the Mac at the nav desk.
  • Works past the breakwater. Cached tide and buoy data keep answering where cell coverage doesn't.
  • A calendar for the chart table. Print the month's tides, ink-friendly, and clip it next to the logbook.

RipCat is planning data, not navigation gear. Predictions are not navigation advice — carry your charts, mind your keel, and make the seamanship calls yourself.
